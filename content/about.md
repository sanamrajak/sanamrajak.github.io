I am a passionate materials and polymer scientist currently in the final phase of my PhD at [IMTEK](https://www.imtek.uni-freiburg.de/), University of Freiburg. I work at the Chair of Chemistry and Physics of Interfaces under Prof. Dr. Jürgen Rühe. I am part of the Collaborative Research Center (CRC) 1537, [ECOSENSE](https://uni-freiburg.de/ecosense/), within Project C2. I developed self-cleaning surfaces with anti-biofouling and anti-soiling properties that keep sensor surfaces clean and functional for months of outdoor exposure. My work lies at the interface of polymer science and surface science. I fabricated these functional thin-film coatings using a range of deposition techniques, such as spin coating, dip coating, spray coating, and doctor blading, followed by C–H insertion crosslinking processes. Furthermore, I characterized these self-cleaning coatings and evaluated their performance using various advanced techniques, including NMR, XPS, SEM, FIB-SEM, TEM, WLI, ellipsometry, AFM, nanotribology, contact angle measurements, and other analytical methods. I enjoy understanding how materials work and how their properties can be tailored to solve real-world problems, which makes this field particularly exciting to me.

I completed my B.Sc. in Chemistry from Tribhuvan University, Nepal, in 2017, where I gained my first hands-on experience in organic, inorganic, and physical chemistry. Afterwards, I worked as a teacher, where I was involved in teaching and learning activities and supervised students in science. In 2020, I moved to Germany to pursue my master's degree.

I completed my M.Sc. in Advanced Functional Materials from TU Chemnitz in 2022, where I studied exciton dynamics in organic semiconductors. I fabricated organic solar cells with different quencher concentrations, followed by photolithography, spin coating, and physical vapor deposition. I analyzed their performance using current–voltage (I–V) and external quantum efficiency (EQE) measurements. Furthermore, I gained my first hands-on experience with XPS through work on layered ferecrystalline heterostructures, where I investigated charge transport, chemical shifts, and the chemical composition of these heterostructures. Additionally, I worked on organic synthesis.
